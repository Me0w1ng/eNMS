Multiprocessing allows work for multiple devices to run concurrently.

Use the Multiprocessing checkbox to enable/disable multiprocessing for a service or workflow. The Maximum Number of Processes field controls the limit of how many devices can run concurrently. Higher concurrency makes sense for operations that spend a lot of time waiting for data from devices. Configure lower concurrency for compute focussed activities. To fine tune your workflow, start with max processes at 15 and test with both higher and lower numbers. Keep in mind that actual performance varies based on other activities running on the same system. For a more detailed view of operational standards regarding multiprocessing and workflows, see our documentation here.

Contexts where multiprocessing adds value

  • Enable multiprocessing on services in a service by service workflow or subworkflow
  • Enable multiproessing on device by device workflows and sub-workflows
Contexts where multiprocessing does not add value
  • Disable multiprocessing on service by service workflows
  • Disable multiprocessing on services or sub-workflows within a device by device workflow
